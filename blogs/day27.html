how do you use stack in this, you can also use vector, both will work similarly. first you have consider the monotonic increasing invarience. keep pushing into the stack/vector until you found a digit which is smaller than the element on the top, then you dive into the while loop and keep removing/popping elements that are bigger than the digit that we are transversing through, dont process the digit if its 0. just push it in the stack...You have to also maintain a variable head which you will compare against digit. because of examples like [3, 0 , 0] suppose you have 2 and k>=3. so you have make the new stack [2] but you cant pop normally cuz 0>2 so by normal method you will get [3,0,0,2] which is wrong. so directly compare head= 3 with new digit eg = 2. and clear the whole stack and make it [2]. thats it. you have to also not maintain a condition stack.size()==1 && digit=='0' which is basically not letting 0 come in first position{head position}.
